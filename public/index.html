💬 Full context 👀 Reading the room 📖 Storytelling
The Twist

Talk is cheap. Prove it.

We're going to test both teams with three challenges where you can't just do it your usual way. Same task, harder mode.

The Rules

How it works

  • Split into two teams: Boys vs. Girls.
  • Each round, your team picks one messenger to complete a task.
  • The rest of the team helps — but only how the round allows.
  • Fastest / most correct team wins the round. Track it on the scoreboard.

Round 1

No Words

The challenge

The messenger gets a secret task — a push-up, a high-five, grabbing something in the room — and has to get a teammate to actually do it, but nobody can talk.

  • ✋ Hands stay flat on the table. No pointing, no drawing in the air.
  • 😐 Only facial expressions and groans / hums allowed.
  • ✅ Agree on one groan for yes, one for no.
  • ⏱️ Beat the clock. Correct = point.
60seconds
Round 2

Heads Up

The challenge

The messenger describes the word on the card so their team says it out loud — without ever saying the word (or any part of it).

  • 🗣️ Talking allowed! Describe, act it out with words, give clues.
  • 🚫 Never say the word, rhyme it, or spell it.
  • ➡️ Got it? Tap Correct. Stuck? Tap Skip.
  • ⏱️ Most correct before time runs out wins.
Got: 0  •  Skipped: 0

60s
Round 3

Written Only

The challenge

No talking at all. The messenger writes step-by-step instructions on paper so a teammate can complete the task — exactly as written.

  • ✍️ Everything goes on the paper. No talking, no gestures.
  • 📄 Teammate must follow the instructions literally.
  • 🎯 Clearest instructions = fastest, most accurate result.
  • ⏱️ Write within the time, then act it out.
120seconds

And the winner is…

The real point: nobody "naturally" communicates better. Great communication is a skill — you listen, you stay clear, and you adapt to what the moment needs. Whether it's a groan, a clue, or a written note, the best teams keep trying until the message actually lands.
